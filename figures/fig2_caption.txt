Figure 2. The Standard Genetic Code (SGC) is beyond the null under both
metrics. (a) Null distribution of Dirichlet energy E across 10⁶ degeneracy-
preserving random codes (grey). The SGC (red line) falls at z = −14.6
(p < 10⁻⁶; 0 / 10⁶ random codes reach the SGC's energy). The null is
generated by permuting amino acid labels across the 61 sense codons while
preserving per-amino-acid codon counts, fixing stops, and pinning AUG → Met.
(b) Same null-hypothesis test for noise distortion D: SGC at z = −16.6
(p < 10⁻⁶; 0 / 10⁶). (c) Joint scatter of (D, E) for a 10 000-point random
subsample from the null distribution (grey points). The SGC (red star) lies
outside and below the null cloud in both metrics simultaneously. The two
metrics are strongly but imperfectly correlated across the null (r = 0.959),
so each provides partially independent evidence; neither suggests the SGC's
optimization is an artefact of the other. Marginal histograms along the top
(D) and right (E) axes reproduce panels (a) and (b) for direct comparison.
