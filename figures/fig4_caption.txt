Figure 4. Wobble-position synonymy is the mechanism by which triplet
architecture buffers single-nucleotide errors. (a) Hamming-1 neighborhood
of GCU (Ala) in the SGC. Each neighbor differs from GCU by exactly one base
(the substituted base is shown in bold in the edge color). The three
position-3 neighbors (GCA, GCC, GCG) all encode Ala and are therefore
synonymous with GCU (blue edges). All six position-1 and position-2
neighbors encode different amino acids (red edges). The wobble position
converts all three position-3 substitutions into silent changes; positions
1 and 2 convert none, for a neighborhood synonymy rate of 3/9 (33 %).
(b) Hamming-1 neighborhood of GC (Ala/Gly), the doublet-projection analogue
of GCU under the RAA10 alphabet. The doublet code has six neighbors (three
at each of its two positions). Only GG (which the majority-vote projection
also assigns to Ala/Gly) is synonymous; the other five neighbors cross class
boundaries. Synonymy rate 1/6 (17 %), with no position providing dedicated
wobble tolerance. This explains the mechanism behind the architecture effect
in Figure 3: the third codon position in triplet codes supplies a systematic
error-absorbing channel that has no analogue in doublet codes, regardless
of alphabet size.
