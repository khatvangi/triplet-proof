Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **"Triplet architecture enables deep error-minimization in the genetic code,"** for publication as an Original Article in *Journal of Molecular Evolution*.

This manuscript addresses a central question in molecular evolution: whether the exceptional robustness of the standard genetic code is solely a property of amino acid assignment within triplet codons, or whether triplet architecture itself enables a deeper level of optimization than shorter coding systems. Using two graph-theoretic robustness metrics on the codon Hamming graph, scored in an 8-dimensional physicochemical space derived by principal component analysis, we compare the standard triplet code with reduced-alphabet doublet and triplet controls under matched randomization ensembles.

The main findings are:

1. The standard genetic code outperforms all 1,000,000 sampled random codes on both Dirichlet energy and single-step distortion.
2. In a factorial comparison separating codon length from alphabet size, the shift from doublet to triplet architecture contributes far more to optimization depth than the shift from 10 to 20 amino acids.
3. The mechanistic basis of this difference is the emergence of a highly synonymous third codon position, which creates a directional error-buffering dimension absent from the doublet projection analyzed here.

We believe the manuscript is well suited to *Journal of Molecular Evolution* because it focuses on a core problem in molecular evolution and genetic code theory, uses a quantitatively explicit evolutionary null framework, and connects a classical question about code optimization to a broader architectural argument about why triplet coding permits a qualitatively deeper robustness regime.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to *Journal of Molecular Evolution*.
